Kick the Stigma

Kappa Sigma Games at Georgia Tech

About the Kappa Sigma Games

When: Saturday, September 26th from 9am-3pm. Registration starts at 8:00am.

Where: Georgia Tech's SAC fields (located next to the campus recreation center)

What: The Alpha Tau chapter of Kappa Sigma is proud to present the second annual Kappa Sigma Games: Kick the Stigma. Teams of 10 will compete in a kickball tournament in addition to three smaller events: corn hole, bocce-ball, and a three-legged relay. In addition to the events, food vendors will be present with a portion of their sales being donated. Suicide prevention organizations such as To Write Love On Her Arms, DSBA, NAMI, Active Minds, and the Counseling Center at Georgia Tech will also be present at the event to provide involvement opportunities and helpful resources to students. All proceeds will benefit the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention.

Why: Our goal is to raise money for the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention, raise awareness of a stigmatized issue among students at Georgia Tech, and provide students with involvement opportunities and helpful resources.

Events

Bubble Soccer

Bubble soccer is a main event and will be occurring all day with a win earning teams (5) points and a tie earning teams (2) points. Two teams of ten will compete against each other in a final 30 minute match.

Kickball

Kickball is also a main event and will be occurring all day with a win earning teams (5) points and a tie earning teams (2) points. Two teams of ten will compete against each other in a 3 inning game (or 30 minute gameâ€”whichever comes first).

Cornhole

Cornhole is a smaller event and will be occurring all day with a win earning teams (3) points and a tie earning teams (1) point. Two players from two separate teams (4 players total) will compete against each other until one team reaches 21 points exactly or 30 minutes passes.

Bocce Ball

Bocce ball is a smaller event and will be occurring all day with a win earning teams (3) points and a tie earning teams (1) point. Four players from two separate teams (8 players total) will compete against each other until one team reaches 12 points or 30 minutes passes.

Three-legged Race

The three-legged relay will be the last event of the day and all teams will compete at once. The winning team will receive (7) points, the second place team will receive (5) points and the third place team will receive (3) points. Relay teams will comprise of 5 pairs of 2 players (10 players total).

Participate

The cost of participation is $200 per team of 10 members ($20 per person). Registration includes a free t-shirt! You may join or create an organization, as well as sign up as an individual to be placed on a random team.
